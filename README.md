# CulinaryGraph

A crowdsourced platform for documenting and sharing region-specific cooking techniques, ingredient stories, and traditional preparation methods.

Unlike recipe websites that focus on complete dishes, CulinaryGraph captures the foundational knowledge that underlies cooking traditions: the techniques, the ingredients, and the cultural context that makes them meaningful.

---

## The Problem

Cooking knowledge is often passed down informally and varies significantly by region, even within the same cuisine. Home cooks, culinary students, and food enthusiasts hold deep knowledge about:

- **Traditional techniques** specific to their regions
- **Where ingredients come from** and when they're in season
- **Why certain methods work** and how they vary across cultures
- **Proper tool usage** and ingredient substitutions that preserve authenticity

This knowledge is scattered across memory, informal documentation, and fragmented online sources. There is no systematic way to capture, organize, and share this structured culinary knowledge while preserving its geographic and cultural context.

---

## The Solution

CulinaryGraph provides a structured platform where users can contribute and explore:

- **Cooking Techniques** — Detailed documentation of traditional methods with their regional variations, prerequisites, and relationships to other techniques. For example, how nixtamalization differs between Oaxaca and other Mexican regions, or how fermentation techniques vary across East Asian cuisines.

- **Ingredient Profiles** — Information about ingredients tied to specific regions, including seasonality, traditional sourcing methods, provenance stories, and culturally appropriate substitutions.

- **Knowledge Connections** — Relationships between techniques, ingredients, regions, and cultural contexts. Users can discover how techniques evolved, how they relate across cuisines, and what makes regional variations distinct.

- **Community Validation** — Contributors can verify, refine, and expand on each other's knowledge, creating a trusted resource that respects the diversity of culinary traditions.

---

## Target Users

| Audience | Needs |
|----------|--------|
| **Home Cooks** | Learn authentic techniques and understand the "why" behind methods |
| **Culinary Students** | Structured knowledge about traditional techniques and regional variations |
| **Food Researchers** | Study culinary history, technique evolution, and cultural food practices |
| **Recipe Developers** | Authentic techniques and appropriate ingredient substitutions |
